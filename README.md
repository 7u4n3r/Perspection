# Perspection

Perspection is active lens provision for discernment development.

It is not passive perception, information delivery, coaching, therapy, or advice. Perspection is the act of helping someone look through a clearer lens so they can distinguish pressure-shaped framing from what is actually happening.

## Core definition

Perspection is interpretive orientation infrastructure.

It creates conditions where clearer observation becomes possible, especially under pressure, confusion, institutional framing, inherited assumptions, or role-conditioned behavior.

The purpose is not to tell people what to believe. The purpose is to strengthen discernment so people can see, choose, and act with more clarity.

## What Perspection does

Perspection helps people:

- identify the frame they are seeing through
- notice patterns that were previously invisible
- separate pressure from reality
- distinguish institutional framing from lived observation
- recover discernment without collapsing into certainty
- choose next actions from clearer visibility

## What Perspection is not

Perspection is not:

- perception alone
- therapy
- coaching
- consulting
- teaching as information transfer
- ideology
- a belief system
- a roadmap
- a demand for contribution
