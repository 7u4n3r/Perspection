## Guided Reflection Prompts for Perspection™

### For Your Perspection Practice

---

### Prompt 1: The Weight of a Story

**Objective:** To understand the real-world cost of an unexamined perception.

1.  Choose one of the "Installed Myths" or negative automatic thoughts you identified during your **Interrogation** practice (e.g., "I am not good enough," "I will always be rejected," "I can't trust anyone").
2.  Now, look back over the last year of your life. Write down **three specific instances** where believing this story directly influenced your actions or decisions.
3.  For each instance, answer: **What opportunity might I have missed, what connection might I have avoided, or what action did I fail to take because I was operating from that perspective?**

This exercise is not about regret. It is about building the motivation for change by clearly seeing the heavy price of living inside an uninspected story.

---

### Prompt 2: The Witness in the Fire

**Objective:** To strengthen your ability to stay in the **Encounter** phase without immediately being pulled into a story.

1.  Find a quiet place where you can sit for five minutes undisturbed.
2.  Bring to mind a current, low-level anxiety or worry. Don't pick your biggest fear; choose something small but persistent.
3.  Close your eyes and simply **notice the physical sensations** this worry creates in your body. Is it a tightness in your chest? A knot in your stomach? A buzzing in your hands? Do not name the emotion; just describe the physical data.
4.  For five minutes, your only job is to be a curious scientist observing these physical sensations. When your mind inevitably jumps to telling a story about the feeling ("This means X is going to happen!"), gently say to yourself, "That's a story," and bring your attention back to the raw physical data. **Just watch.**

This practice builds your "observer" muscle, making the **Inspection** phase of the Trinity Method™ easier and more natural over time.

---

### Prompt 3: The First Step of a New Story

**Objective:** To connect your new **Integration** (perspective) to a tangible, real-world action.

1.  Review the "Perspective Reframing" exercise you completed in the workbook.
2.  Look at the "New, More Useful Perspective" you crafted.
3.  Now, brainstorm **five small, concrete actions** you could take in the next 48 hours that are perfectly aligned with this new perspective. The actions should be so small that they feel almost effortless.
    *   *Example:* If your new perspective is "My voice deserves to be heard," a small action could be "unmuting myself in a Zoom call even if I don't speak," or "sharing one idea with a trusted colleague."
4.  Circle the **easiest** action on your list. **This is your only task.**

This exercise proves to your nervous system that a new way of being is not only possible but safe. It makes the new perspective real by translating it into a lived experience, which then seeds the next, more confident **Encounter**.
